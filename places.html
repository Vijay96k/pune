Tourist Places in Pune

Pune, often called the "Oxford of the East," is one of Maharashtra’s most vibrant cities. It is known for its rich Maratha history, educational institutions, pleasant climate, and a perfect blend of traditional and modern culture. The city was once the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire and still holds great historical importance.

Top Attractions

  1. Shaniwar Wada
  2. Sinhagad Fort
  3. Aga Khan Palace
  4. Pashan Lake
  5. Lavasa City

Detailed Information

  • Shaniwar Wada: Built in 1732, it was the seat of the Peshwas. Known for its massive gates and historical significance.
  • Sinhagad Fort: A historic hill fort famous for the battle of Tanaji Malusare and trekking views.
  • Aga Khan Palace: A symbol of India's freedom struggle where Mahatma Gandhi was imprisoned.
  • Pashan Lake: A peaceful lake ideal for bird watching and nature lovers.
  • Lavasa City: A planned hill city known for scenic beauty and modern infrastructure.

Best Time to Visit Pune

The best time to visit Pune is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and ideal for sightseeing. Monsoon season (June to September) is also beautiful for nature lovers due to lush greenery.
